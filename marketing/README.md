# marketing/ — draft campaign artifacts

Draft, un-published marketing content for Duhem. **Nothing here is live.** These
are campaign artifacts staged for review; the act of publishing them is a human
decision (see *The gate* below).

## Provenance

Produced by **Kirzner** (the AI business operator, `onsager-ai/kirzner`) — its
Marketing expert, executing one opportunity end-to-end. The opportunity came from
a live, web-grounded *alertness scan* of Duhem's distribution landscape:

> **Teardown demo** — "watch an AI agent write 200 green tests that prove nothing,
> then watch Duhem catch the bug," posted to HN / r/ExperiencedDevs / QA
> newsletters.
>
> *Why now (from the scan):* the "agents grade their own homework" critique
> crystallized into shared, quotable language in mid-2026 (tautological
> assertions, tests that re-implement the code, mocked-away logic, coverage
> without behavior), QA outlets are publishing AI-test review checklists, and no
> one has shipped the definitive side-by-side demo — while Duhem already owns the
> runnable proof (`demo/self-masking/`).

Kirzner did the research, design, and drafting; this PR is the executed output.

## The gate

This is where the loop stops on purpose. Everything up to here is done; the
remaining steps are the founder's signature:

1. **Review** the write-up and posts — do they read as *you*?
2. **Decide** where the write-up is hosted (recommended: your blog) and fix the
   links in `launch-posts.md`.
3. **Publish / post** from your own accounts. Kirzner does not post on your behalf,
   spend, or speak publicly as you without this sign-off.

Merging this PR is not "go live" — it's "these drafts are good enough to keep."
Publishing is the separate, deliberate act.
